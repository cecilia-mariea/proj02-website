Proposition: For a variety of countries, the carbon footprint of bank loans is decreasing or steady over time.

FOR the Proposition

Design Decisions and Rationale:

  • Group countries by development level and taking the average. Removes visual cluter, but still tells retains interesting context of the countries.
  • Use normalized values (values at 2015 = 100) to abstract variation. Also use the 2015-2018 subset to utilize the normalization to 2015 as a deceiving 'ceiling'.
  • Scale the y-axis to start 87.5 to make the decreasing slope sharper, emphasizing the time trend.
  • Use a line plot to represent time-series data. Use annotations to quantify the decrease such that the reader does less visual work to quantify themselves. Still, the sharp slopes are still draw attention.

AGAINST the Proposition

Design Decisions and Rationale:

  • Use non-normalized emission intensities to reveal increasing carbon footprints without comparing to 2015 levels.
  • Use per country values, without grouping, to remove the effect of averaging trends of low emitters.
  • Only represent a subset of coutries present in the dataset to remove visual clutter and emphasize increasing trends.
  • Use annotations to quantify increased carbon footprint to emphasize increasing emissions. This is especially useful for the bottom four countries of the top 5 emitters since they are closely grouped.
